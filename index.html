Our Main Objective

Established in 1999, Monorien is a leading exporter of processed timber to different parts of the world. We are committed to providing high quality timber products to meet the needs of the construction and building industries. Our mission is to provide the best quality timber products to help build and maintain strong and durable structures. We have invested heavily in the latest procedures, which allows us to produce high-quality timber products that are tailored to the specific needs of our customers. Our timber is sourced from sustainable forests and is processed using the latest processig technology and machinery. We place great importance on the quality of our products and ensure that they are of the highest standard. We also ensure that our timber is treated properly and that the environment is not harmed in the process...

Are you in need of timber hauling services? If so, then you’re at the right place. Timber hauling is one of the most important tasks for a timber company and has to be done safely and effectively. It requires careful planning and skillful execution, which is why engaging with an experienced provider can make all the difference between success and failure. In this blog post we will explore what makes up a good timber hauling service – from its equipment to hiring working professionals – as well as offer advice on how to select the best partner for your project. First off, it’s essential that any contractor providing timber hauling services deploy appropriate vehicles for their work: those specifically designed or modified to carry logs across terrain without risking damage both during transport or upon eventual delivery at sawmills or other sites where wood products are made.
Secondly, experience matters when recruiting workers who have knowledge about logistics optimization (such as working out optimal loading orders based on weights and sizes), safe driving practices in rough terrains (rough roads can lead them astray)and legal compliance relating e safety standards set by governing bodies such as OSHA (Occupational Safety & Health Administration).
